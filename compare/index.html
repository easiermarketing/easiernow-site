What you keep

Easier Now does these as well, so moving costs you none of them.

  • Tasks Quick capture Type a task in plain language and it is in.
  • Tasks Deadlines A due date on the card, purple until it passes, then red.
  • Projects Work grouped into projects Every task sits under a project, and a project under a client.
  • Notes Notes on the work A rich text box on every task: images, bullets, code blocks.
  • Tasks Repeating work Routines daily to yearly. Miss one and it waits at the top of tomorrow.
  • Time Tasks and meetings in one calendar Two-way Google Calendar sync, with a verb prefix on every task.
  • Time Hours on a task Every task carries an estimate, so the day can be counted.
  • Team Hand work to someone Assign a task to a teammate on the Team plan.
  • Team Your team can see the work Shared projects, and where each one has got to.
  • Projects This one after that one Finish-to-start dependencies, and the schedule respects them.
  • Time Work placed in the gaps Flexible work fits around the things you marked strict.
  • Time Refit the day when you run late Press Optimise schedule and what is left is refitted.
  • Time Long work cut to fit A task too long for the gap in front of it is sliced into pieces.
  • Time Told what will not fit The hours are counted against the day you actually have left.
  • Tasks One task on screen One card and four buttons, not a list to sort through.
  • Tasks The order decided for you Urgency, importance and pay grade set what comes next.
  • Tasks A missed week, one card at a time Catch-up hands back each overdue task: Skip, Do today or Done.
  • Time Hours recorded without a timer Finish a task, confirm the minutes, and it is logged.
  • Time Estimates that learn Your real minutes make the next estimate a better one.
  • Notes Checkboxes become real tasks Tick-boxes in a note turn into cards, after you confirm each one.
  • Team Work and personal kept apart A teammate cannot see what you do after hours.
  • Tasks Points you can switch off Finished work grows a tree. Turn it off and nothing is lost.
